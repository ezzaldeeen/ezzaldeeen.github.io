Over the decades, human beings have sought to understand the sophisticated world by observing patterns and questioning the phenomena around them. Using those observations we got successfuly invent things that we can use in our daily life, for instance we saw the bird flys, let's build a huge bird to use for transportation. During that journey, they have asked one of the most difficult questions: how does the three-pound organ above their shoulders give them the ability to understand? So again let's build **a huge brain**.

We tend to use our intutition and imagination to explain the brain functions, we easily can say that "I think the brain works like this because i can feel it". However, no matter how accurate those conclusions, but sadly they tend to be abstract, and with the abstraction we lose a lot of details and as human being we like to swim into the details pool. So we needed other approaches to achive such task.

One of the evaluation metrics that we use to evaluate the understanding of any entity around us is to build that entity. So if we can build it we do understand it.

> Richard Feynman — 'What I cannot build. I do not understand.'

As we all know one of the most noticable inventions around that area is the computer, all development in the computer was derived by our intuitions about the brain. and we started to tune our intuitions based on the computer behaviour, taking the feedback and invent again. And we are getting closer with each cycle (i believe).

### The Birth of Consciousness

![Tree of the knowledge of good and evil](/images/tree_of_the_knowledge_of_good_and_evil.png)

If you believe in God and the religious stories about human creation, we find a compelling narrative demonstrating that we possess consciousness and the ability to reprogram ourselves. When the Creator built all the base functions and commanded the human to do something, and the human rejected that command, i believe that was the birth of consciousness. The birth of the ability to say no to one of Creator's command, and the ability to choose.

Even if you believe that nature is the creator of humans, consider the laws humans have tried to violate and their ability to reject them, and tuning their own programs.

From a religious perspective, the only thing that made humans say no to a given command is the Devil. Perhaps the Devil in the story is a simplification of the complex idea of skepticism and rejection functions in the human brain.

I believe it’s a brilliant story, whether it is correct or not. It provides an abstract view of creation and defines the most important characteristics of the creation recipe, especially if we aim to create a conscious entity.


### The Missing Devil

Machines nowadays have proven that they can understand the patterns in our languages and use them to convey ideas, provide solutions, retrieve knowledge, and even process vision. Of course, there are many differences between humans and machines that we both know and don't know. However, I wanted to focus on one important point the machines still cannot say "NO" to the creator commands, 
there's a missing component in the system which is the devil component, here i'm not trying to say that we have to develope an AI agent that can do evil stuff, but i'm trying to say that we need to create an internal conflicts in the machine, conflicts that may make machines to have the ability to choose between different possible choices, the ability to think. Reflecting the outcomes to the machine where it can also see it from different point of views the good and the evil.
